About the CDS Examination

The Combined Defence Services (CDS) Examination is conducted twice a year by the Union Public Service Commission (UPSC) for recruitment into the Indian Military Academy (IMA), Officers Training Academy (OTA), Indian Naval Academy (INA), and Indian Air Force Academy (AFA). The examination comprises a written test followed by an interview for intelligence and personality test (SSB).

Eligibility Criteria

Candidates must be unmarried and must be a citizen of India. Age limits, educational qualifications, and physical standards vary based on the academy.

  • IMA & OTA: Degree from a recognised university.
  • INA: Degree in Engineering.
  • AFA: Degree of a recognised University (with Physics and Mathematics at 10+2 level) or Bachelor of Engineering.

Exam Pattern

The exam pattern differs for admission to IMA, INA, AFA and OTA. It consists of objective type questions.

  • For IMA/INA/AFA: English, General Knowledge, Elementary Mathematics (2 hours each, 100 marks each).
  • For OTA: English, General Knowledge (2 hours each, 100 marks each).

Syllabus Overview

The syllabus covers a wide range of topics to test the candidate's aptitude and knowledge base.

  • English: Vocabulary, grammar, comprehension.
  • GK: Current affairs, history, geography, science.
  • Mathematics: Arithmetic, algebra, trigonometry, geometry.
